Sara Stokes Patterson

Neuroscience Ph.D. Candidate at the University of Washington.

I am a Neuroscience Ph.D. candidate in the Neitz lab at the University of Washington in Seattle. I study color-coding retinal circuits in the primate retina. My research combines 3D reconstructions of retinal neurons from serial electron microscopy with single cell electrophysiology, computational models and human psychophysics.

In the fall, I will begin a postdoc studying foveal ganglion cell function with adaptive optics and calcium imaging in the lab of Dr. David Williams at the University of Rochester.

Before graduate school, I spent a year as a post-bac at the NIH studying zebrafish cone photoreceptor development in the lab of Dr. Ralph Nelson. I graduated from Dickinson College with a B.S. in Neuroscience.
